PROJECT ABSTRACT 
 
The Mumma Meals Management System is a comprehensive web-based platform designed to 
promote and educate consumers about dehydrated food products. This system serves as an 
online product catalogue and recipe hub, offering detailed product information, nutritional 
benefits, and diverse culinary applications. It features a user-friendly interface with category-
wise product listings, interactive recipes, and a secure review system accessible only to verified 
buyers. The administrative backend provides efficient product management, inventory control, 
order placement, digital invoicing, and sales analysis. Developed using HTML, CSS, 
JavaScript, PHP , MySQL, and Python, Mumma Meals aims to address the growing demand for 
sustainable and nutritious food options while laying the groundwork for future e-commerce 
integration.

INTRODUCTION 
 
 Motivation 
In an era of rapid urbanization and evolving dietary habits, dehydrated foods 
have gained popularity due to their extended shelf life, compact storage, and 
nutritional benefits. However, many consumers lack knowledge about their 
diverse applications. Mumma Meals aims to bridge this gap by creating a 
comprehensive digital platform that showcases these products alongside 
curated recipes, promoting awareness and engagement. 
 
 Problem Statement 
Consumers often struggle to find detailed information on dehydrated food 
products, their nutritional benefits, and their culinary applications. Existing 
platforms lack a seamless, engaging user experience that integrates product 
information and recipes. Businesses need a structured product management 
system with features for inventory tracking, order processing, and customer 
engagement. 
 
 Objective 
The project aims to provide an extensive catalog of dehydrated food products, 
complete with detailed descriptions and health benefits, to educate and inform 
users about their nutritional value. Additionally, it will feature a diverse recipe 
repository that showcases the versatility of these products, encouraging users 
to explore creative and delicious ways to incorporate them into their meals. A 
key focus is promoting health-conscious and sustainable eating habits by 
highlighting the long shelf life, reduced waste, and nutrient retention of 
dehydrated foods. The platform will be designed with a visually appealing and 
intuitive user interface to enhance user experience and engagement. 
Furthermore, the system will be structured to accommodate future e-
commerce functionality, allowing seamless integration of online purchasing 
options as the project evolves.

 Project Scope and Limits 
 
Scope: 
 
1. Centralized Product Management 
Organizes products into categories with detailed descriptions, images, 
ingredients, and recipes. Provides a search and filter option for easy 
navigation. 
2. Enhanced Customer Engagement 
Offers detailed product information to educate users about product 
usage. Includes a review and rating system to gather customer feedback. 
Allows admins to maintain a loyal customer base by providing 
personalized service. 
3. Admin-Controlled Order Processing 
Admins place orders on behalf of customers and generate invoices. 
Customers receive invoices via email, ensuring proper record-keeping. 
4. Efficient Inventory & Sales Tracking 
Inventory is monitored manually to prevent stock outs. Generates 
automated sales reports (daily, weekly, monthly, annual) for business 
analysis. 
5. Brand Building & Information Management 
Provides an ‘About Us’ and ‘Contact’ section to improve business 
credibility. Offers product-based YouTube video tutorials to promote the 
brand. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
8 | P a g e  
 
Limitations: 
 
1. No Direct Customer Ordering 
Unlike e-commerce platforms, customers cannot place orders directly. 
Instead, the admin manually processes orders. 
2. Limited Payment Integration 
The system currently does not support online payment gateways like 
UPI, credit cards, or PayPal, requiring external payment processing. 
3. No Multi-Vendor Support 
The platform is designed only for a single dried food company, not a 
marketplace with multiple sellers. 
4. No Mobile App Integration 
The system is a web-based platform with no dedicated mobile 
application, which might limit accessibility for mobile users. 
5. Limited Role-Based Access Control 
All admins have full control, meaning there are no sub-admin roles like 
product managers or sales executives.

SYSTEM ANALYSIS 
 
EXISTING SYSTEMS 
Dried food businesses primarily rely on manual order processing, using calls, 
WhatsApp, or emails, leading to mismanagement. Invoices and inventory are 
tracked via Excel or physical records, causing stock errors. Customer 
engagement is limited, with no proper access to product details, recipes, or 
reviews. Sales tracking is manual, making reporting tedious. Additionally, the 
absence of a centralized online presence restricts product visibility and 
marketing relies solely on social media. 
SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS OF EXISTING SYSTEMS 
 
Scope: 
 
1. Manual systems provide flexibility but at the cost of efficiency. 
2. Traditional sales tracking allows customized pricing, but it's prone to 
errors. 
3. Small businesses avoid technical complexity, but they lack automation. 
 
Limitations: 
1. Time-Consuming – Manual order processing and inventory tracking are 
slow. 
2. Human Errors – Risk of incorrect stock updates and mismanagement. 
3. Lack of Customer Interaction – No review or feedback system for 
improvement. 
4. No Data Insights – Businesses lack automated reports for growth 
analysis. 
5. Limited Scalability – Cannot handle large order volumes efficiently.
